    Good Carbohydrates vs. Bad Carbohydrates

    Good Carbohydrates vs. Bad Carbohydrates

    Good Carbohydrates Bad Carbohydrates
    Whole grains (brown rice, quinoa, oats) Refined grains (white bread, white rice)
    Fruits and vegetables Sugary drinks and snacks
    Legumes (beans, lentils) Pastries and sweets
    Nuts and seeds Processed snacks (chips, crackers)
    High-fiber foods Low-fiber foods

    Good Carbohydrates: These are typically high in fiber and nutrients, and they digest more slowly, which helps to keep blood sugar levels stable.

    Bad Carbohydrates: These are often processed, high in sugar, and low in nutrients. They digest quickly, causing rapid spikes in blood sugar levels.
